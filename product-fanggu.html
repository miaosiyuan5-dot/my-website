About the Antique Round Teapot

The Antique Round (仿古圆壶) draws its form from Song-dynasty bronze vessels — a flattened, wide-shouldered body that excels at cooling tea for everyday enjoyment. Its generous 350ml capacity suits sharing, whether at a quiet breakfast or an extended tea gathering with friends.

A perennial bestseller in our collection, this teapot balances accessibility with genuine craft: the wall thickness is even throughout, the pour is clean and controlled, and the lid sits with a satisfying fit.

Purple Zisha (Zini)

Sourced from Huanglongshan, Yixing, Jiangsu. This dark reddish-brown clay has a high iron content that gives it exceptional heat retention and promotes the gradual development of a rich patina with use.

Purple Zisha is excellent for oolongs, puerh, and black teas, as its microporous structure moderates temperature and mellows astringency over time. The wider body of the Fanggu form allows heat to dissipate slightly faster than narrower pots — ideal for teas that open up best at around 90°C.

Eight Stages of Making

  • Clay preparation — raw ore crushed, levigated, and aged
  • Slabbing — clay beaten into uniform slabs of even thickness
  • Body forming — paddle-and-anvil technique; the wider body requires extra care to maintain even wall thickness throughout
  • Spout, handle, and lid attachment — each joined with slip and smoothed
  • Slow drying — air-dried in shade to prevent cracking
  • Engraving (selected pieces) — motifs carved at leather-hard stage with steel tools
  • Firing — single firing at approximately 1180°C in a gas kiln
  • Final inspection — each piece inspected and sealed by the artisan

Caring for Your Teapot

First-use seasoning (开壶): Simmer the new teapot gently in water for 20–30 minutes to open the pores, then rinse with your chosen tea several times before regular use.

Daily cleaning: Rinse with hot water only — no soap or detergent, which would strip the developing patina. Empty spent leaves promptly after each session.

Drying: Leave the lid off after use so the interior air-dries fully. Store in a well-ventilated spot away from strong odours.

Patina: Buff the exterior lightly with a clean cloth while the teapot is still warm from use. Over months and years of this care, the clay develops a deep, lustrous patina — the hallmark of a well-loved pot.
