About the Xishi Teapot

Xishi teapot is one of the most revered round-form teapots in the Zisha canon. Its feminine, rounded silhouette is said to mirror the beauty of the legendary Xi Shi. The body is shaped by hand using the paddle-and-anvil method, then engraved before firing.

Each piece bears the artisan's personal seal — a mark of individual authorship that distinguishes Master Edition work from all other series. No two engravings are alike.

Purple Zisha (Zini)

Sourced from Huanglongshan, Yixing — the world's most prized deposit of Zisha ore. The clay fires to a rich, dark reddish-brown with a subtly granular surface texture.

High iron content gives Purple Zisha exceptional heat retention and promotes the development of a deep, lustrous patina over years of use. The unglazed interior gradually absorbs the essence of the teas brewed within, enhancing flavour with every session.

Eight Stages of Making

  • Clay preparation — aged Zisha ore is processed, wedged, and rested
  • Slabbing — the clay is beaten into uniform sheets
  • Body forming — paddle-and-anvil shaping of the rounded form
  • Spout, handle, and lid attachment
  • Slow drying to prevent cracking
  • Hand engraving of the decorative motif
  • Single firing at approximately 1180°C in an electric kiln
  • Final inspection and seal application

Caring for Your Teapot

First-use seasoning (开壶): Simmer the teapot gently in plain water for 20 minutes, then brew a pot of your chosen tea and discard it. Repeat once more. This opens the clay pores and removes any residual kiln dust.

Daily use: Rinse with hot water only — no soap or detergent. The unglazed clay absorbs flavour; soap will taint future brews.

Drying: After use, leave the lid ajar and allow the teapot to air-dry fully before storing.

Patina: Buff the exterior gently with a soft cloth while the teapot is still warm. Over time, the clay develops a deep, living sheen that no factory finish can replicate.
