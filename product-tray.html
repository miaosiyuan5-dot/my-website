About the Engraved Teapot Tray

The Engraved Teapot Tray (壶承) is the unsung hero of the tea table — it collects overflow water, frames the teapot, and completes the aesthetic. Hand-formed in Beige Duanni clay and engraved with a bamboo motif, it is a practical and beautiful addition to any gongfu tea set.

Each tray is made from a single slab of clay, shaped by hand at the leather-hard stage. The bamboo engraving varies subtly from piece to piece, ensuring every tray is unique.

Beige Duanni (段泥)

Duanni is a mixed-mineral clay that fires to a pale, warm buff tone. Unlike the denser purple Zisha, Duanni has a slightly lower density that allows the tray to absorb and release moisture slowly — making it ideal for use as a drip tray during the tea session.

Its natural beige colour provides a beautiful contrast to the darker tones of purple Zisha teapots, tying the tea table together. The clay is sourced from Yixing and shares the same geological origin as the clays used for our teapots.

How the Tray Is Made

  • Clay preparation — Duanni ore processed and aged for workability
  • Slab forming — clay rolled and cut to the tray dimensions
  • Edge shaping — walls and rim shaped by hand while clay is soft
  • Bamboo engraving — pattern carved into the surface at leather-hard stage
  • Slow drying — air-dried to prevent warping
  • Firing — single firing at approximately 1100°C
  • Final inspection — flatness and finish checked by the artisan

Caring for Your Tray

Daily use: Rinse with hot water only after each tea session. The tray is designed to absorb small amounts of water and will dry naturally between uses.

Patina: With regular use, the tray develops its own patina alongside your teapot — the two pieces will season together and take on a harmonious warmth over time.

Storage: Allow to air-dry fully before storing. Keep away from strong odours, which the porous clay can absorb.
